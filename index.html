Made after the release of 'Star Wars: A New Hope,'' this documentary serves as a sneak peak into the methods that Lucas and the other producers employed to make the movies. Some of the key features that were highlighted in this documentary were...
  • The creative techniques used to create some of the shots
  • George Lucas’s vision for the movie and the rest of the Star Wars universe
  • The reactions of the cast to the creation of the film
  • The way that John Williams and the music in the film shaped it into what it became
  • The use of sound design in places like the light saber fight to enhance the film
  • The influence of mythology on the world building in the movie

  • One example of this is the layering of different visuals to create one cohesive shot. The synthesis of shots with the actors, the Millennium Falcon, the death star, and the star field all came together one on top of each other to create “the shot”
  • In the words of Carrie Fisher, the actor for Princess Leia, “Making Star Wars was a lot like the movie, you never knew what was going to happen next”
  • The magic of the film from the actors point of view came from trusting the editors and camera work bring together all the individual shots and pieces to become the final product
While this documentary does discuss a variety of the “behind the scenes” aspects of the first movie, there are certainly some topics that it failed to address...
  • The documentary doesn’t provide an in-depth of how fans reacted to and interacted with the first Star Wars movie
  • The reaction to the movie that kicked off the rest of the Star Wars world provides critical context for the following movies and TV shows
  • Diversity and representation are also not addressed through this documentary
  • The role of Princess Lei as the lone major female character and the lack of diversity in the cast is not covered
